A Strange but Useful Behavior

In Belgium, people have been spotted carrying ironing boards to parks, street parties, festivals, and raves.

They are using them as quick outdoor tables — for drinks, food, bags, phones, and a shared group surface.

It looks funny. The product logic is real.

Why It Works Outdoors

An ironing board already has outdoor-product DNA:

  • Fold-flat frame
  • Adjustable height
  • Long, narrow surface
  • Mostly metal construction
  • Perforated metal / mesh top under the fabric cover

For outdoor use, the fabric cover is removed or ignored.

That leaves a durable metal structure that can shed rain or spilled beer instead of pooling liquid.

The insight is not “make a party ironing board.”

It is: a fold-flat, height-adjustable metal utility surface can do more than ironing.

What the Mechanism Gives Us

Useful strengths:

  • Flat-folding storage
  • Fast setup
  • Multiple height positions
  • Simple metal tube construction
  • Familiar low-cost lock architecture

Design risks:

  • Side-load instability
  • Pinch points
  • Small feet on soft ground
  • Accidental collapse
  • Tipping under uneven loads

The work is to keep the simplicity and fix the outdoor failure points.

Camp / Field Utility Board

Lead concept.

A hard-top, height-adjustable work surface for camp prep, fishing, hunting, gardening, range work, tailgating, and wagon-side organization.

This is the cleanest Westfield translation.

Not a better party table.

A better portable outdoor work surface.

07 / 15

Built for Real Outdoor Work

Core features:

  • Wipeable or perforated metal top
  • Raised edge or modular tray insert
  • Oversized feet for grass, dirt, sand, and gravel
  • Positive lock with clear open/closed feedback
  • Hooks for tools, towels, bags, or lanterns
  • Fold-flat carry profile
  • Wagon-compatible storage or attachment

The product should feel like portable outdoor infrastructure — credible at a campsite, pier, garden, range, or tailgate.

Fold-Flat Social Side Table

Third concept. Closest to the original behavior.

A compact outdoor side table using the same fold-flat, height-adjustable leg logic.

Built for chairs, beach, patio, camp, coolers, and tailgate use.

Key features:

  • Narrow drink/snack/phone surface
  • Cupholder or bottle groove
  • Multiple height positions
  • Anti-tip feet
  • Strap or handle
  • Chair, cooler, or wagon compatibility

What We Need to Prove

  • Does the leg geometry stay stable on grass, sand, gravel, and pavement?
  • What foot design prevents sinking and tipping?
  • What lock prevents collapse and pinch points?
  • What folded thickness is acceptable?
  • Can it store on or inside a wagon?
  • Can existing Westfield tubing, hinges, feet, or table parts be reused?

The next step is not styling.

The next step is mechanism proof.
