Shade Sail System
The Concept
Three
overlapping
triangles.

Triangular sail geometry is inherently rigid under tension and more wind-stable than rectangular panels. Three overlapping sails with staggered anchor points provide full patio coverage with no single point of failure.

  • ①
    Two fixed corners — wall anchors or post tops, bolted permanently. Set-and-forget tension via turnbuckles.
  • ②
    One live corner — stainless cable runs through a pulley at the post top, down to a deck-mounted manual winch. Crank down to lower, crank up to raise.
  • ③
    Overlap design — the three sail live corners are on separate posts, so no two winches share a structure. Partial coverage maintained if one sail is lowered.
3 sails · 3 winch posts · full patio coverage

System Detail
Storm Prep
The Winch Mechanism
Lower in minutes,
not hours.
Raised — normal operation
  • All three sails taut and overlapping
  • 2 fixed corners tensioned via turnbuckles — never touched
  • Live corner held at height by winch lock
  • Full patio coverage, rain and UV protected
Lowered — storm prep (~5 min/sail)
  • Back off fixed-corner turnbuckles slightly
  • Release winch pawl, crank live corner to deck level
  • Bundle sail and strap to post
  • Repeat for remaining sails — done

Hardware: standard Lewmar or Harken marine winch, deck-mounted at post base. Stainless cable over a single-sheave block at post top. All off-the-shelf marine rigging — no custom fabrication required.

One person · no tools · storm-ready in under 15 minutes

A US commercial shade contractor would quote $15,000–$30,000 installed for a comparable retractable system. This approach — direct-sourced fabric + marine rigging — achieves the same result for roughly 80% less.
  • Shade fabric — 3 panels (Colordura, ex-factory + container share)
    $800–1,500
  • Steel posts + base plates (3 posts, local fab)
    $600–1,200
  • Winches × 3 (marine grade, deck mount)
    $450–900
  • Cable, pulleys, turnbuckles, hardware
    $400–700
  • Wall anchors + masonry install
    $300–600
  • Concrete footings + post install (labor)
    $800–1,500
  • Total Estimated Range
    $3,350–6,400
