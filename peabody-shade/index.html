The brewery's outdoor patio previously used two temporary frame tents for shade and rain protection. A recent storm destroyed them. Rather than replace like-for-like, this proposal outlines a permanent, storm-resilient upgrade — one that can be lowered quickly when weather threatens.

Shade Sail System
The Concept
Two
overlapping
triangles.

Triangular sail geometry is inherently rigid under tension and more wind-stable than rectangular panels. Two large overlapping sails cover the full patio area, anchored to the existing steel I-beam roof structure on one side and the reinforced wooden pergola on the other.

  • ①
    Steel I-beam anchors — existing roof structure on the building side provides solid fixed anchor points. No new structural steel required.
  • ②
    Reinforced pergola anchors — existing wooden pergola structure on the opposite side, reinforced at anchor points to handle sail tension loads.
  • ③
    One live corner each — stainless cable through a pulley down to a deck-mounted marine winch. Crank down in ~5 min before a storm, raise and re-tension after.
2 sails · I-beam + pergola anchors · full patio coverage

System Detail
Storm Prep
The Winch Mechanism
Lower in minutes,
not hours.
Raised — normal operation
  • All three sails taut and overlapping
  • 2 fixed corners tensioned via turnbuckles — never touched
  • Live corner held at height by winch lock
  • Full patio coverage, rain and UV protected
Lowered — storm prep (~5 min/sail)
  • Back off fixed-corner turnbuckles slightly
  • Release winch pawl, crank live corner to deck level
  • Bundle sail and strap to post
  • Repeat for remaining sails — done

Hardware: standard Lewmar or Harken marine winch, deck-mounted at post base. Stainless cable over a single-sheave block at post top. All off-the-shelf marine rigging — no custom fabrication required.

One person · no tools · storm-ready in under 15 minutes

A US commercial shade contractor would quote $15,000–$30,000 installed for a comparable retractable system. This approach — direct-sourced fabric + marine rigging — achieves the same result for roughly 80–85% less.

    $600–1,200
  • Steel posts + base plates (3 posts, local fab)
    $600–1,200
  • Winches × 2 + pulleys (marine grade, deck mount)
    $450–900
  • Cable, pulleys, turnbuckles, hardware
    $400–700
  • I-beam clamps + pergola reinforcement hardware
