The Situation
Storm damage
took the tents.

The brewery's outdoor patio previously used two temporary frame tents for shade and rain protection. A recent storm destroyed them. Rather than replace like-for-like, this proposal outlines a permanent upgrade — a tensioned shade sail system that provides both sun and rain protection, and can be lowered quickly when severe weather threatens.

Shade Sail System
The Concept
Two
overlapping
waterproof sails.

Triangular sail geometry is inherently rigid under tension and more wind-stable than rectangular panels. Two large overlapping sails provide full sun and rain protection across the patio. Anchored to the existing steel I-beam roof structure on the building side and the reinforced wooden pergola on the other, with no new structural posts required.

  • ①
    Steel I-beam anchors — existing roof structure on the building side provides solid fixed anchor points. No new structural steel required.
  • ②
    Reinforced pergola anchors — existing wooden pergola structure on the opposite side, reinforced at anchor points to handle sail tension loads.
  • ③
    One live corner each — stainless cable through a pulley down to a deck-mounted marine winch. Crank down before severe storms, raise and re-tension after. For everyday rain: leave raised — the waterproof fabric handles it.

System Detail
Storm Prep
The Winch Mechanism
Lower in minutes,
not hours.
Raised — normal operation
  • All three sails taut and overlapping
  • 2 fixed corners tensioned via turnbuckles — never touched
  • Live corner held at height by winch lock
  • Full sun and rain protection — waterproof fabric handles everyday weather
Lowered — storm prep (~5 min/sail)
  • Back off fixed-corner turnbuckles slightly
  • Release winch pawl, crank live corner to deck level
  • Bundle sail and strap to post
  • Repeat for remaining sails — done

Hardware: standard Lewmar or Harken marine winch, deck-mounted at post base. Stainless cable over a single-sheave block at post top. All off-the-shelf marine rigging — no custom fabrication required.

One person · no tools · storm-ready in under 15 minutes

Wind & Load
Wind Loading
Proven in
commercial
applications.

Tensioned shade sails are widely used and approved in commercial settings — restaurants, hotels, stadiums, and breweries across the country, including in high-wind coastal regions. Well-engineered systems handle Baltimore's conditions comfortably.

Baltimore's ASCE 7 design wind speed is 90 mph. Wind pressure on sail fabric runs 5–15 lbs per sq ft depending on speed and angle. Cable tension can spike to 3,000+ lbs under gusts — all well within the capacity of properly specified hardware and anchor points.

Tension & Hardware Spec
Tension & Pitch
Pitch does
most of the
heavy lifting.

Corner pre-tension at rest: 150–400 lbs per corner — well within the capacity of off-the-shelf marine winches and rigging. The single biggest design lever for keeping loads manageable is sail pitch.

The Pitch Rule

A steeper pitch dramatically reduces corner tension. A 30° pitched sail sees 3–4× less load than a flat one under the same wind. Staggering the I-beam anchor heights above the winch corners isn't just about drainage — it's the most effective load-reduction tool available.

Target: 20–30° minimum pitch on both sails
Hardware Spec
Component Minimum Spec
Wire rope (cable) 1/4" 7×19 SS 316 — ~7,000 lb break strength
Turnbuckles 1/2" jaw-jaw, SS 316
Winch (per sail) 800–1,200 lb working load (e.g. Lewmar 40/48)
Corner D-rings SS 316, min. 5,000 lb WLL
I-beam clamps Beam clamp rated for point load — get PE sign-off
Pergola reinforcement Steel gusset plates + 1/2" lag bolts into structural members

Pergola note: If a site assessment finds the existing wooden pergola can't meet the required tension loads, the straightforward fix is to attach steel posts to the masonry wall directly behind it — providing a solid, code-compliant anchor without changing the visual footprint.

Structural engineer review recommended — a PE sign-off letter on the anchor points is ~$500–1,000 and gives the brewery owner confidence and city compliance.
