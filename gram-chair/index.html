GRAM Chair

Lightweight rocking through parallelogram motion — compliant mechanism innovation on a director's chair platform

Westfield Outdoors · Innovation S28 · Concept Stage

The Concept

Director's Chair Meets Compliant Mechanism

  • Built on a standard director's chair frame — proven, cost-effective platform
  • Elastomeric corner joints (shown in red) replace rigid hardware at all four corners
  • Under weight shift, corners flex — frame transitions from square to parallelogram
  • The geometry change itself creates the rocking arc
  • No runners. No springs. No linkages. Just material flex.
The Name
GRAM — a dual reference to the chair's lightweight construction (lighter than any existing rocking solution) and the parallelogram motion that creates the rocking arc.
Mechanism

Square → Parallelogram = Rocking Motion

Neutral (square)
→
Rocking (parallelogram)
● Red corners = elastomeric compliant joints that flex under load
  • Elastomeric material (TPE/TPU) at each corner — overmolded or insert-molded
  • Provides tuned resistance — stiff enough to support, flexible enough to rock
  • Mechanical stops integrated into corner geometry — prevents over-rotation forward and back
  • Self-centering — stored strain energy in the elastomer returns frame to neutral
  • The corner joint is simultaneously pivot + spring + stop — one component, three functions

Key Insight
Every competitor adds components to a chair to make it rock — runners, springs, linkages, bases. GRAM takes the opposite approach: redesign the joints so the frame itself creates the motion. Fewer parts, lighter, cheaper.

Patentability Assessment

A Design We Can Own and Defend

✅ Novelty — STRONG
No prior patent discloses elastomeric corner joints in a portable folding chair frame as the rocking mechanism. The square-to-parallelogram deformation approach is unprecedented.
✅ Non-Obviousness — MODERATE–STRONG
Counter-intuitive approach. All prior art teaches adding components for rocking. GRAM redesigns the joints — opposite direction from conventional thinking. KSR analysis favorable.
Lead Independent Claim (Draft)
A portable folding chair comprising a chair frame having left and right side members connected at four corner joints, each comprising an elastomeric element configured to permit controlled angular deflection under load, wherein deflection of the corner joints transitions the frame from a substantially rectangular configuration to a parallelogram configuration, thereby enabling rocking motion, and wherein the chair frame is collapsible to a folded storage condition.
6 claims drafted covering: broad mechanism, mechanical stops, self-centering, director's chair platform, elastomer material spec, and method claim.
IP Strategy

Protecting the GRAM Mechanism

🔥 File Provisional — Now
Establish priority date before any public disclosure, prototype sharing, or trade show presentation. Est. cost: $1,500–$3,000 with patent counsel.
Broad Claim Scope
Claim 1 covers any portable folding chair using elastomeric corner joints + parallelogram deformation for rocking. Not limited to director's chair platform.
Design Patent Potential
Distinctive visual appearance of the elastomeric corner joints may support design patent coverage in parallel with utility filing.
Prior Art to Distinguish
US4,371,142 (Homecrest, 1983 — expired): Flexible sheet between separate chair and base for rocking. Key differences: GRAM is portable/folding, corners are integrated (no separate base), parallelogram deformation ≠ rotation relative to a base.
⚠️ Confirm ITC Case
Verify outcome of GCI ITC case 337-TA-1178 vs Westfield (2019, asserting US9,282,824 + US10,874,217). GRAM's different mechanism should clearly differentiate, but any consent orders should be reviewed.
Risks & Open Questions

What We Need to Prove

Material Performance
  • Elastomer fatigue life under cyclic rocking loads
  • UV resistance for outdoor use — TPE/TPU degradation profile
  • Temperature range — cold stiffening vs. heat softening
User Experience
  • Tuning shore hardness for the right "feel" across weight range
  • Rocking amplitude and frequency — does it feel natural?
  • Stability perception — users need to feel secure
Mechanical Design
  • Stop design — robust limits without pinch points
  • Fold integration — how corners interact with fold/deploy
  • Corner joint attachment to metal frame tubes
Manufacturing
  • Overmold vs. insert-mold tooling cost
  • Elastomer material sourcing with Haibo / HF
  • Assembly line integration — new process vs. standard chair line
