The trip was most valuable as an alignment and execution reset, not as a single breakthrough event. Materials training, charter reviews, supplier interaction, and samples working sessions all fed into one concrete outcome: each tracked program left with a clearer next-round sampling path.

Meeting arc

The schedule moved from foundational context into program-specific working sessions, then into recap and collaboration alignment.

  • Materials and tubes introduction, plus process training, opened the trip.
  • Tent, future softgoods, innovation, and furniture charter meetings established shared priorities.
  • A combined supplier visit / samples working session on Apr 17 became the operational center of the trip.

Program status

Samples were brought for Hawkeye V2, Gram Chair, FlowMotion V2, Swing Chair, and the Daypack project.

  • HF is retaining Gram Chair, FlowMotion V2, and Swing Chair samples for continued development.
  • HF is returning Hawkeye V2 and Daypack samples.
  • All five programs had productive reviews and now have updated sampling plans.
