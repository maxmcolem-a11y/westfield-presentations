04 / Technology

The living hinge.

A living hinge is a thin, flexible zone within a rigid material — typically formed by reducing cross-section thickness along a crease line.

PP & HDPE are proven materials — high-flex polymers used in living hinge applications since the 1950s. Billions of fold cycles tested.

Perforation pattern controls flex angle — slot geometry determines the folding direction, radius, and return force.

Origami geometry adds rigidity — the same way a corrugated sheet resists bending, a folded polygon locks under load without a frame.

No fasteners, no loose parts — the hinge is integral to the material. Nothing to lose, nothing to break.

Skunk Works Risk

Known unknowns.

These are the open engineering questions Sprint 1 is designed to answer.

Full-Scale Mechanism

The fold pattern proven at small scale may not translate to a full-size 20"×36" wagon. Geometric forces multiply — wall stiffness, hinge torque, and corner lock-up all change with scale.

High Risk

Long-Term Hinge Durability

Living hinges in PP are excellent for bottle caps and lids (<100 cycles). Wagon use implies hundreds to thousands of fold cycles over years — fatigue behavior at this cycle count requires validation.

Medium Risk

Load-Bearing Geometry

The origami structure must support 100+ lbs static load while maintaining panel rigidity. Initial FEA is needed before physical prototyping to de-risk geometry choices.

Medium Risk

Perforation SRP

The slot-and-relief perforation pattern that enables flexure must be manufacturable within standard tooling SRP. Tolerance control on thin hinge zones is a key process variable.

Low Risk
