Accounting Basics

The two financial statements that drive everything

Section 1 — Accounting Basics Westfield Outdoors

Income Statement

Measures profitability over a period of time (month, quarter, year)

Line ItemAccount
Gross Sales (Revenue)4105
− Product Cost (COGS)5105
= Gross Profit
− Operating Expenses
= Net Income
Key idea: The Income Statement resets each period. It answers: "Did we make money?"

The Golden Rule of Accounting

For every transaction, the total debits must equal the total credits. NetSuite enforces this automatically.

Remember: Assets & Expenses increase with debits. Revenue, Liabilities & Equity increase with credits.

When we invoice a customer, NetSuite creates balanced journal entries behind the scenes:

AccountDebitCredit

4105 Gross Sales$100

COGS Entry (if item costs $40)

AccountDebitCredit

Section 2 — Balanced Entries Westfield Outdoors

What This Means for You

  • You don't create journal entries manually — NetSuite does it when you enter transactions (invoices, bills, receipts)
  • But understanding them helps — when something looks wrong in a report, you'll know which accounts to check
  • NetSuite won't save an unbalanced transaction — if you get a "transaction is not in balance" error, check your amounts
You enter the business event. NetSuite handles the accounting math.

Records vs Transactions

Permanent data vs. actions that move money

Section 3 — Records vs Transactions Westfield Outdoors

Two Types of Data in NetSuite

📋 Records

Permanent reference data — the "nouns"

  • Customers
  • Items / Products
  • Vendors
  • Employees
  • Locations
Records exist. They don't affect financials by themselves.

⚡ Transactions

Actions that move money — the "verbs"

  • Sales Orders
  • Invoices
  • Purchase Orders
  • Item Receipts
  • Vendor Bills
Transactions happen. They create journal entries and affect financials.
Section 3 — Records vs Transactions Westfield Outdoors

How They Work Together

Transactions always reference records. Records provide the data; transactions create the accounting.

Think of it this way: A Customer record is like a contact card. A Sales Order is the action of selling them something.

Section 4 — Transaction Flows Westfield Outdoors

Purchasing: Bill & Payment

Vendor Bill

The vendor's invoice to us. If receipt already posted to AP, the bill matches against it.

If received without a PO:
Debit 1210 Inventory (or Expense)
Credit 2010 Accounts Payable

Bill Payment

When we actually pay the vendor.

Debit 2010 Accounts Payable
Credit Cash/Bank Account

Key insight: Receiving inventory and paying for it are separate events. The Item Receipt adds to inventory; the Bill Payment removes cash.

Section 4 — Transaction Flows Westfield Outdoors

Transfers, Returns & Adjustments

Inventory Transfer

Moves stock between locations

Debit 1210 Inv (To)
Credit 1210 Inv (From)

Total inventory stays the same — just moves between warehouses.

Return (RMA)

Customer returns product

Reverses the original sale: reduces AR, adds back inventory, reverses COGS

Creates a Credit Memo or Return Authorization.

Inventory Adjustment

Corrects quantity discrepancies

Debit/Credit 1210 Inv
Offset to 6820 Inv Adj

Used after physical counts or damage write-offs.

Section 5 — Item Records Westfield Outdoors

Cost Concepts

Understanding the different cost fields on an item

Purchase Price

— what we pay the vendor

Set on the PO. Can vary by vendor or order.

Average Cost

— what NetSuite uses for COGS

Calculated automatically. Weighted average of all purchase prices for units in stock.

Transfer Cost

— intercompany pricing

Used when transferring items between subsidiaries or internal divisions. May include markup.

Section 5 — Item Records Westfield Outdoors

How Average Cost Works

NetSuite recalculates average cost with each receipt

EventQtyUnit CostOn HandAvg Cost
Receipt #1100$10.00100$10.00
Receipt #2100$12.00200$11.00
Sell 50 units−50—150$11.00
Receipt #350$14.00200$11.75
Why this matters: When we fulfill an order, NetSuite uses the current average cost to calculate COGS (5105). A higher average cost = lower margin on each sale.

Section 8 — System Structure Westfield Outdoors

Departments

Organizational units that help segment reporting

What Departments Do

  • Segment financial reports by team
  • Track revenue & expenses per group
  • Control approvals and workflows
  • Filter dashboards and saved searches

Example Departments

  • Sales
  • Product Design & Development
  • Operations / Warehouse
  • Marketing
  • Finance / Accounting
Set on transactions and employee records. When you enter a PO, the department tells NetSuite which team is spending the money.

Locations

Physical or logical places where inventory is stored and transactions occur

What Locations Track

  • Inventory quantities per location
  • Fulfillment origin for shipping
  • Cost tracking by warehouse
  • Transfer orders between locations

Example Locations

  • Indianapolis HQ / Warehouse
  • 3PL Warehouse (East)
  • 3PL Warehouse (West)
  • In-Transit
On every transaction: Location determines where inventory is added (receipts) or removed (fulfillments). The same item can have different quantities at different locations.

Key Takeaways

  • Every transaction creates balanced journal entries automatically
  • Records are your reference data; Transactions are your actions
  • Inventory is the bridge between purchasing and sales
  • Department, Class, Category, Location = your reporting dimensions
  • Clean data in records = accurate financials downstream
