Timber Ridge 26L daypack development

The mandate is a recognizable Timber Ridge silhouette, not just another daypack.

The workstream is aimed at a structured 26L pack with one ownable signature move, tuned for the designed middle and balanced between trail credibility and everyday carry.

26L target volume
$79 to $100 designed middle
Trail to office crossover
15 inch laptop sleeve

The core mandate

Design an iconic, highly structured silhouette anchored by one signature element visible from across the room.

The market position

Deliver a premium-feeling aesthetic without drifting out of Timber Ridge’s value-conscious zone.

The aesthetic and function

Clean, athletic, intentional, with laptop and quick-access utility, while avoiding blobby or over-tactical reads.

Narrative

Early exploration is about stance, aspect ratio, and structural attitude.

This phase is deliberately freed from pattern constraints. The goal is to discover raw silhouette families first, then use physical and visual filtering to narrow the field.

What the slide needs to say

Volumetric control becomes the headline, not a hidden mechanism.

The technical story here is the semi-rigid top and bottom ring structure working with concertina side panels and an adjustable strap system that preserves force-free lid access at any compression state.

Ring architecture

Semi-rigid top and bottom rings stabilize the opening and preserve the body geometry through compression.

Concertina body

Side panels create a visible, mechanically legible method of volume reduction instead of relying on crushed fabric.

Mechanical advantage

The adjustable strap system permits lid access without fighting the compression state, which is the key differentiator.
