Timber Ridge daypack

Three concept directions, simplified.

This version uses real extracted source images from the sketch and spec PDFs, plus the provided red compression-pack image as the compression reference.

Deck structure: Shape 1, Shape 2, Shape 3, compression precedent, and a brief-aligned direction check.

Images extracted directly from DaypackSketches_01.pdf and TimberRidgeDaypack_Specs_041626.pdf
01
Shape 1

Most angular and visibly structural.

  • The front graphic reads as a bold X-frame across the body.
  • The concept feels the most technical and outdoors-forward of the three.
  • Best if the goal is a strong identity from a distance, even at the cost of some softness.
Source: extracted image from DaypackSketches_01.pdf, page 1
02
Shape 2

Cleaner sweep, softer stance, stronger zipper story.

  • The diagonal path feels more continuous and less segmented than Shape 1.
  • The silhouette keeps the faceted influence but reads more approachable.
  • Best if the goal is a balance between concept character and everyday usability.
Source: extracted image from DaypackSketches_01.pdf, page 2
03
Shape 3

Most rectilinear and closest to a clean commercial daypack.

  • The panel layout is simpler, calmer, and easier to read quickly.
  • The geometry still carries the concept DNA without feeling overworked.
  • Best if the goal is a fast path from sketch language into a manufacturable product.
Source: extracted image from DaypackSketches_01.pdf, page 3
04
Compression reference

Useful precedent: body compression, not just lid compression.

  • The accordion sidewall makes volume change visible and product-defining.
  • Compression happens in the body architecture instead of only pulling down from the top opening.
  • This is the cleanest external reference for a more novel compression story.
Source: user-provided red compression pack image
05
Brief check

Current brief direction already looks closest to Shape 3, with room to borrow a little more attitude from Shape 1.

Front

The current front view is already cleaner and more productized than the earlier sketch options.

Back

The back panel and shoulder system are aligned with a practical daypack target.

Open

The panel-load opening stays the clearest functional story in the current brief.

Simple take: Shape 1 has the strongest graphic identity, Shape 2 is the softest transition, and Shape 3 is the cleanest product path. If this needs to move fast, keep the current brief direction and selectively borrow compression cues from the red precedent rather than rebuilding the whole bag around it.
